1.4

Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why? One of the main reasons a digital project can fail is due to insufficient time management and over-scoping. This frequently occurs regardless of the size of the project, even with experienced companies. This will cause features of the project to be cut or additional man-hours will need to put into the project, increasing the time and or cost in order to complete it. Other reasons involve not fully understanding the limitations of the technology being used, and unexpected technology problems, ranging from internet not working up to server and hardware crashes. Insufficient testing can also cause a product to be released in a bad and buggy state, or even fail completely at a later stage.

Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects? The collection and storing of personal data and sensitive personal data under the GDPR rules is a lot more restrictive than before. Requiring controllers and processors to have a specific purpose in order to store data rather than just, we might need it at some point. There are huge financial fines for not complying with these rules, both for small and large companies. Data collection must be a done in a transparant way, requiring customers consent for a lot of data collection, which is most impactful on social media platforms, such as facebook or google. In addition out of date or innacurate data must be erased or updated as soon as is feasibly possible. In general when designing a product try and reduce the data collected to what is needed, and relevant to the product itself. https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/guide-to-the-general-data-protection-regulation https://www.monterail.com/blog/introduction-to-gdpr-for-digital-companies
